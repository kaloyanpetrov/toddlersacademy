Dear Parents,

Congratulations on choosing Toddlers Academy! We are proud to offer a bilingual program featuring both the Oxford International Program and the Bulgarian curriculum, officially approved by the Bulgarian Ministry of Education. We understand that selecting the right kindergarten is not an easy decision. By choosing Toddlers Academy, you have entrusted your child to a kindergarten that has undergone thorough authorization processes and demonstrated a strong commitment to professionalism and high-quality education recognized internationally.

Our staff adhere to strict professional and ethical standards. We provide ongoing, high-quality training to ensure that our teaching reflects the best practices in early childhood education.

Education, however, extends beyond the classroom. We encourage all parents to take an active role in their child’s learning journey. Simple activities at home—such as reading a bedtime story or counting cars of a certain color on the way home—can make a big difference.

This website offers an overview of our Mission, Philosophy, and Approach to teaching, as well as how we incorporate internationally developed methods to serve your child’s best interests.

We sincerely hope that your family and child will enjoy all the wonderful opportunities our kindergarten provides. If you have any questions or need further information, please do not hesitate to contact us. We look forward to hearing from you.

Yours sincerely,

⭐ Our Mission

At Toddlers Academy Kindergarten, children thrive in an environment built on mutual respect, creativity, curiosity, and discovery. We believe early childhood development flourishes within a calm atmosphere of love and support.

Our goal is to give children the freedom to express themselves while nurturing them into responsible, independent, and critical thinkers. We ensure that children are active participants in their growth: they experience rather than observe, and create rather than imitate.

📅 A Day at Toddlers Academy

Morning Routine

  • 08:00 – 09:00 | Arrival & Logic Games: A warm welcome in our communal area. Children transition to their classrooms at 8:30.
    • Handover Policy: For safety, a child is only officially accepted when handed over personally to a teacher or authorized staff member.
  • Morning Circle: We begin with “Circle Time,” using multi-sensory techniques (music, movement, and visuals) to cater to unique learning styles.
  • 09:00 – 09:30 | Breakfast: A varied and nutritious start to the day.

Academic & Active Play

  • 09:30 – 11:30 | Core Curriculum & English: 30-minute modules covering Ministry of Education standards, creative arts, music, and English immersion.
  • 11:30 – 12:00 | Active Play: Time to relax, move, and recharge.
  • 12:00 – 13:00 | Lunch & Etiquette: A relaxed meal focusing on independence, healthy habits, and table manners.

Afternoon & Departure

  • 13:00 – 15:30 | Rest & Recovery: A quiet break to calm the nervous system and restore energy.
  • 15:30 – 16:00 | Afternoon Snack: A healthy boost from our curated weekly menu.
  • 16:00 – 17:30 | Afternoon Enrichment: Further educational modules and creative activities.
  • 17:30 – 18:30 | Supervised Play & Pickup: Supervised indoor/outdoor games until final departure at 18:30.

📋 General Policies

Departure & Transitions

  • Authorized Pickup: Children are only released to parents or explicitly designated third parties.
  • Daily Updates: Teachers provide brief updates on achievements or concerns during the afternoon handover.
  • Personal Care: Parents must provide clean spare clothes in labeled bags. Reusable water bottles should be taken home every Friday for sanitization.

Parent Communication

  • Student Progress Map: Used during parent-teacher conferences to track mastery of skills.
  • Weekly Curriculums: Sent by Lead Teachers to outline themes, projects, and special events.
  • Monthly Portfolios: Creative work folders are sent home on the last Friday of each month.

What to Bring

  • Non-slip indoor shoes/slippers.
  • A full set of spare clothes (including socks and underwear).
  • Note: Please label all items. Valuable jewelry and toys that cause disruption or safety risks should remain at home.

🎓 Educational & Social Philosophy

  • Thematic & Project-Based Learning: We use deep-dive themes and long-term projects to allow children to explore topics according to their own interests.
  • Bilingual Environment: Communication happens in both English and Bulgarian, allowing children to learn “language in action” during daily routines.
  • Conflict Resolution: We teach children to resolve conflicts peacefully. If needed, a “Reflection Space” (Thinking Chair) is used to help a child regain composure and discuss the situation with a teacher.
  • Safety & Dignity: We maintain a zero-tolerance policy regarding humiliation, threats, or harm to any child.

📅 Health, Safety, & Finances

  • Nutrition: Balanced meals provided by a licensed catering service.
  • Health Policy: Sick children cannot be admitted. A medical note is required for re-entry after a prolonged illness. No medication is administered without written parental consent.
  • Security: No child is ever left unsupervised. We rely on parents for personal handovers to ensure total safety.

Financial Terms: Fees and conditions are governed by the Educational Contract signed prior to the school year.

Medical Documents

The health of our children and teachers is our top priority at kindergarten. We believe that a safe and healthy environment is essential for happy learning and growth. To ensure this, we strictly follow all the guidelines and instructions provided by the Ministry of Health. By doing so, we create a caring space where every child and staff member can thrive with confidence and peace of mind. Your child's well-being is at the heart of everything we do!

Required data upon admission: Although paper documents are no longer needed, parents must ensure a visit to the general practitioner to have the following current data entered the system:

  • Health prevention card: Contains information about the child's general condition, height, weight, and specific needs.
  • Immunization status: Data on all mandatory vaccines according to the immunization schedule.
  • Microbiological tests:
    • For pathogenic intestinal bacteria and parasites: Must be done no earlier than 15 days before admission.
    • Blood and urine tests: (Only for nursery groups) Must be done within one month before admission.

Requirements for child absence: According to the new rules and the current Regulation No. 3 on health requirements:

  • Over 30 days: For absence due to epidemic indicators, a single negative test result for intestinal bacteria is required.
  • Over 2 months: A mandatory single negative test result for intestinal parasites is required.
